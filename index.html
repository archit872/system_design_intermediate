System Design - Intermediate Level

This book turns beginner system-design fluency into applied, production-grade skill. You'll learn to design scalable, reliable, and evolvable systems, evaluate trade-offs with metrics, and deliver a complete capstone architecture.

About this Book

This curriculum is for software engineers who have completed a Beginner System Design course. It emphasizes practical design decisions-how to translate constraints into architectures, quantify reliability, and iterate safely from prototype to production.

Outcome: Confidently design and explain systems with measurable SLAs/SLOs, clear consistency choices, robust failure strategies, and cost-aware performance plans.

How to Use

  • Sequence: Read Chapters 1->8 in order, then complete Chapter 9 (Capstone) and Chapter 10 (Pitfalls & Next Steps).
  • Depth markers: Each chapter includes worked examples, comparisons, and exercises ("Checkpoints").
  • Artifacts: Maintain a living design doc; update SLOs, capacity estimates, and diagrams each chapter.
  • Glossary: First uses of terms are linked; refer to the Glossary as needed.

Quick Start

  • Skim Chapter 1 to align on goals, metrics, and a design workflow.
  • Pick a capstone track (video streaming, marketplace, or social feed) to carry through the book.
  • Create a one-page "Design Ledger" (assumptions, constraints, risks, open questions). Update it every chapter.

Capstone Tracks Chapter 9

Pick one project and carry it across chapters. At each step, apply the chapter's focus to refine your design:

  • Video Streaming Platform: Focus on write-once, read-many patterns, CDN strategy, and multi-region storage.
  • E-commerce Marketplace: Partitioning, consistency choices (orders vs inventory), and idempotent workflows.
  • Social Media Feed: Fan-out on write vs read, ranking pipelines, and hot-key mitigation.

Study Rhythm

  • Weekly cadence: 1-2 chapters + checkpoint exercise.
  • Artifacts: Design doc, capacity sheet (QPS, P95/99 latency targets), reliability plan (SLO + error budget), cost notes.
  • Peer review: Present trade-offs; defend choices with SLIs and back-of-envelope capacity math.
